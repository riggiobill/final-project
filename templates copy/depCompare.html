Department Comparisons

What motivates people to stay or leave, to put their whole hearts into their work or to get by, various individually, but our data shows some interesting trends by department. Logistic regression analysis indicates that predictions about retention of sales people relies more heavily on gender and years with the current manager, while predictions about R&D are weighted more heavily towards years in the workforce and years since last promotion. This is not to say that the other group does not care about these factors, but that their responses to them do not appear statistically to motivate their decisions as much as their feelings about some other factors.

The collection of charts below show how the three departments compare on several important employment factors. While they may differ in how they weigh the importance of each factor in their decision-making, nevertheless there are some common trends. In all cases, for example, turnover is greatest in the first six years of employment and steadily declines as tenure and time in role increase.
